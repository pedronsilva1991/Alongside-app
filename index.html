Frequently Asked Questions

Will my hair fall out?

Hair loss is a common side effect of Cytarabine. It usually begins two to three weeks after treatment and grows back after treatment ends. Glofitamab rarely causes hair loss. Your team can discuss scalp cooling and wig services.

When will I feel sick?

Nausea most often starts within the first 24–48 hours after treatment. Take your anti-sickness tablets as prescribed, even if you feel well. Contact the hospital if you cannot keep fluids down.

How long is treatment?

A Cytarabine session typically takes 4–6 hours including observation. Glofitamab follows a step-up dosing schedule over several visits; early doses involve longer monitoring.

Can I drive?

Do not drive on treatment days — you may feel tired or unwell afterwards. Arrange transport in advance. Ask your team when it is safe to drive between cycles.

Can I work?

Many patients continue some work between cycles, but fatigue is common. Plan lighter duties around treatment days and speak to your team about what is realistic for you.

When should I call hospital?

Call immediately for fever, bleeding, chest pain, breathing difficulty, severe diarrhoea, confusion or a worsening rash. If in doubt, call — do not wait.

What is Nadir?

Nadir is the point 7–14 days after treatment when your blood counts are lowest and infection risk is highest. Check your temperature daily and call the hospital immediately if you have a fever.

What is CRS?

Cytokine Release Syndrome is an immune reaction that can happen after Glofitamab. Symptoms include fever, chills, low blood pressure and a fast heartbeat. Seek urgent help if these occur.

What is ICANS?

ICANS is a rare effect on the nervous system after immunotherapy. Watch for confusion, speech problems, tremor, difficulty writing or drowsiness — call the hospital urgently.
